Marketing Campaign Analysis

Objective
The objective of this project was to analyze marketing campaign performance and identify customer characteristics and behaviors associated with higher conversion rates.

Dataset
Customer marketing campaign dataset containing 2240 customers, including demographic, behavioral, and purchase data.

Tools Used
SQL Server Management Studio (SSMS)
SQL

Key Findings

Overall conversion rate was 14.9%.

Customer spending behavior was the strongest predictor of conversion:
- High spenders: 41.9% conversion rate
- Medium spenders: 14.6%
- Low spenders: 9.8%

Customers with frequent prior purchases were significantly more likely to convert:
- High purchase frequency: 23.4%
- Low purchase frequency: 6.7%

High-income customers converted at twice the rate of lower-income customers.

Business Impact

These findings suggest marketing campaigns should prioritize:

- High-spending customers
- Customers with strong purchase history
- High-income customers

Targeting these segments can significantly increase conversion rates and marketing ROI.
